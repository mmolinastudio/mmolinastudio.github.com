Journey

2014-06-04 general, futurejs, conference, UK

Since two months ago, when my work at OHIM finished, I have had something like a holidays. Every day reading, watching films, series, enjoying my friends, making the most of my gym... and of course, sometimes, programming. But many times I wasn't able to find a motivation... until last 1st may, when I went to the FutureJS Conference in Barcelona with my friends Germ√°n and Joaquin -who was also teaching a workshop there-. Those days I had the opportunity to meet great people who inspired me and made me think about the way I want to take -thanks David and Joanne, hope to see you again one day-. Then I decided what would be my higher priority just in this moment of my life. Speaking with many people in Barcelona, I didn't feel any time confortable with my English oral fluency, and I realized I had to change it. That's why the last week, on 27th of May I left to Liverpool.

Firstly the idea was to work with a team of young artists of the city who needed a website and help with marketing (social networks). But finally, they leave me behind and my first week of this journey was very different than I expected. I was five days in Liverpool without any kind of plan, sleeping in two hostels and knowing people from all around the world. It was being funny but also I was getting burned in few days so I looked for something different in Workaway. I left Liverpool to come here, to a farm in the middle of the country of Lincolnshire, close to a little town called Wainfleet all Saints. I'm loving and enjoying this quiet and peaceful place. Of course, I have to work sometimes, but the work is not hard. Most of the things I have done are heping with computers, fixing bikes and sometimes also helping with the horses, goats, chickens, rabbits, ducks and lots of small animals. I'm learning lots of things, and luckly, they give me many free time. I don't know what I will be doing in a week or two. Who cares? I want to visit many cities and this is the best moment to do it. I need this time for myself... and what is more important, little by little, I'm feeling more confident with the language.
